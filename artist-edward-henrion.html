Edward Henrion
Artist

Edward Henrion (1928-2016) grew up in Detroit Michigan. After studying painting with Alexander Zlatov- Mirsky in Chicago, he moved to New York in 1949 and graduated from the Cooper Union College of Arts & Sciences in 1952. He was a resident of Greenwich Village with his wife and fellow Cooper Union graduate, Marilyn Henrion, for 65 years. A sharp-eyed chronicler of the '50's and '60's 'beat' scene as well as world events in his satirical drawings, he also produced an impressive collection of exquisite life drawings of nudes. The extensive legacy of his drawings and paintings is now administered by his widow, Marilyn Henrion. With a background in art and philosophy and a keen eye for the absurdities of the human condition, Edward Henrion was an equal opportunity offender who created satirical artworks that took no prisoners.
